Introduction

According to legend, King Dan was the first Danish king in the 4th century - thence the name Danmark, Dan's borders.
Since Gorm the Old times (936 a.c) til 2018, Denmark has always had a royal in power. There has been 8 royal dynasties in total: Gorm, Fairhair, Estridsen, Bjelbo, Pomerania, Palatinate-Neumarkt, Oldenburg and Glucksburg. History can be considered as a large deck of cards, to which every year a new card is added. As you scroll down through this website you will reveal curiosities from the oldest dynasty (Gorm) to the most recent one (Glucksburg).

Danish Royals have occupied the throne for 1082 years . The following graph shows the year percentage out of 1082 that the four main dynasties have been in power.

Gorm Dinasty

The Gorm dynasty is also known as house of Knýtlinga (House of Canute's Descendants).
The kings in this house are definitely linked with an incipient unification of Denmark.





Christianity is introduced

The son of Gorm the Old, Harold Bluetooth, completed building the Jelling Stones (a detail of one of the stones is shown on the right) in honour to his parents Gorm and Thyra. The Jelling stones are known as Denmark's birth certificate.
Although Christianity gained a foothold in Denmark during the reigh of Gorm the Old, it was not until his son Harold I Bluetooth was baptised in 965 that Christianity was set in Denmark as the main religion.





Harold BLUETOOTH

Bluetooth is a wireless technology standard for exchanging data over short distances; the word bluetooth comes from the initial letters in nordic runes of the danish king Harold Bluetooth.



Roskilde Cathedral origins

Harold Bluetooth built a church in Roskilde, where he was later buried. The site where this primitive church was, is nowadays occupied by Roskilde Cathedral, where numerous memebers of the danish royal family are buried.




An English Nightmare

Sweyn Forkbeard, Harold Bluetooth's son, secretly hired a man called Palnatoke in order to kill his father.
Sweyn Forkbeard became king of England in 1030 after conquering a good deal of England.
The famous song "London Bridge is falling down" comes from the day the Londoners burnt the bridge over the river Thames to halt the advance of Sweyn and his viking crowd.



A poisoned toast

Intrigues are always floating around of royalty in the same way as in "Game of thrones".
Harold III Hardicanute, great great grandson of Gorm the Old, died suddenly at a wedding in London, where he proposed a toast to the bride and then dropped dead. This resulted in the dissolution of Hardicanute III's realm, returning England to the Anglian kings.

Estridsen Dinasty

Denmark's royal house was at its height during the time of the Valdemars, when her influence extended throughout most of the Baltic region, and again during the reign of Queen Margrete I.







Copenhagen flourishes

Valdemar I the Great developed a powerful Crown. Due to the fact that he was paying tribute to the German Emperor Friedrich Barbarossa, he decided to fortify Denmark's southern border and established several towns such as Havn (later Copenhagen) in case the germans stop the peaceful relationship with Denmark. Around 1160, Valdemar the first gave to Absalon, Bishop of Roskilde, the control over Copenhagen.



Danish flag origin

The Danish flag or Danneborg is the oldest continuously used national flag. According to the legend the flag fell from the sky at the Battle of Lyndanisse (in Estonia) in 1219 during the reign of Valdemar II the Victorious. Around this period Denmark was larger than ever in terms of area.



Knight of the Holy Sepulcre

Valdemar IV Atterdag was a diligent politian but also a brutal man. The conduct of his army was savage, even for that time period. This monarch visited the two popes: one in Rome and the other in Avignon. Valdemar decided to travel to Jerusalem and was made a Knight of the Holy Sepulcre before returning to Denmark in 1371.

Kalmar Union

Destiny wanted Queen Margrete I to play an important role in history. After the death of her husband and her only son, and as Denmark had at the time no provision that enabled a woman to rule in her own right, Margrete secured herself the position of Regent and was appointed as "Denmarks's Wife and Husband and Mighty Guardian of the Entire Realm". In 1397 Margrete was able to unite the three Scandinavian kingdoms in a personal union under one crown for her successor Eric of Pomerania by the Kalmar Union.


The impostor son

Prince Olaf of Denmark, son of Margrete I, died suddenly at the age of 16. According to some rumors coming from detractors of the Queen, young Olaf did not died and escaped to Prussia. Some merchants found a young boy that looked like the dead prince, and decided that the young boy had to impersonate the rightful heir to the throne. Queen Margaret heard the rumours and said that if the peasant boy could prove he was her son, she would gladly accept him. Fortunately, Margrete easily discovered that the "candidate" was not her son, as he could not even speak danish. He was condemned to be burned at the stake with all the notes begging for mercy around his neck.

Oldenborg Dynasty

The Oldenburg dynasty is one of Europe's most influential royal houses, with branches that rule or have ruled in Denmark, Iceland, Greece, Norway, Russia, Sweden, Schleswig, Holstein, and Oldenburg.





Party bloodbath

In 1520 the danish king Christian II made the Swedes to surrender after a conflict. Swedes representatives sworn their allegiance to Christian II during a bunch of festivities held at the Stockholm Castle lasting several days. On the fourth day, the gates to the castle were closed and all the detractors of Christian II were bestially executed. This event is known as the "Stockholm bloodbath".



Lutheranism is here!

Christian III was a pro-lutheran monarch that after "convincing" all the danish realms, he had three Catholic bishops arrested and set Lutheranism as the main religion of the kingdom. Martin Luther sent the king his congratulations.


Building the realm

Christian IV was among the greatest builders in the Nordic countries, and despite military disasters (i.e fiasco in the Thirty Years war), economic recession, and marital horrors (i.e infidelities and affair intrigues), Christian stands as a leading, loved and admired king. He established towns that include Christianshavn, Kristiania (new Oslo) among many others. He also left his taste throughout Copenhagen, such as Rosenborg Castle, Borsen, the round tower, and Trinitatis Church etc.

The first absolute monarch

Christian V was the first to accede to the throne as absolute monarch. During his rule the absolutist social structure was completed, and the power of the royal household was not under any threat.

Queen Caroline Matilda of Great Britain was the wife of Christian VII. Johann Friedrich Struensee was the king’s personal physician. s. Constitution

The new Constitution for Denmark was signed by Frederik VII on 5 june 1849, bringing to an end 187 years of absolute monarchy with the following words: "The King will not attend the Sovereign in Council unless invited to do so".

Glucksburg Dynasty

The Glücksburg dynasty complete name is Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg, is a Dano-German branch of the House of Oldenburg.
Margrethe II of Denmark, Harald V of Norway, Constantine II of Greece, Queen Sofía of Spain and Charles, Prince of Wales are patrilineal members of cadet branches of the Glücksburg dynasty.





Resistance to occupation

Christian X was a king that won popularity of the danish people by daily riding his horse through the streets of Copenhagen during the Nazi occupation of Denmark. The King's ride became a symbol of Danish sovereignty. According to the story he used to wear the Star of David during this parades.



An Olympic love

When Crown Prince Frederick of Denmark travelled to Sydney, Australia, for the 2000 Summer Olympic Games, he could not have imagined that in a pub in Australia he would meet his future wife, Mary Donaldson. Crown Prince Frederick later said he felt like she was his soulmate when they met. After a few time dating Prince finally confessed who he was to Mary; and the rest is history.



Thinking in the afterlife

Both Queen Margrette II and her husband Henrik already have a fancy coffin to lie in their final resting in peace. The coffin consists of transparent glass and it stands on 3 columns of Danish granite, Faroese basalt and Greenland marble.
However, two shapes are made to lie inside the grave, so that the real royal bodies can be buried below.
